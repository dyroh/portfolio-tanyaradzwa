About Me

Hi, I’m Tanya, a freshman at Lewis & Clark College studying Computer Science and Mathematics. I’ve always been drawn to the intersection of logic and creativity, and I enjoy working on projects that challenge me to think deeply while building something meaningful. I’m especially interested in how technology can be used to solve real-world problems in thoughtful and impactful ways. Whether it’s designing a website, working with data, or exploring new technical ideas, I enjoy the process of learning, experimenting, and gradually improving my skills. I’m still growing as a developer, but I value building things that are both functional and intentional. A big part of my academic journey has been developing strong analytical thinking through math, coding, and problem-solving. At the same time, I like balancing that with creativity—thinking about how design, user experience, and storytelling all play a role in the way people interact with technology. Outside of academics, I’m someone who really values community, creativity, and expression. I enjoy sports, music, and art, and I’ve been involved in leadership roles and group activities that have shaped how I communicate and collaborate with others. These experiences influence how I approach my work—I try to stay open-minded, reflective, and grounded in what I’m creating. Right now, I’m focused on continuing to learn, build, and explore different areas within tech. I’m especially curious about where data, systems, and real-world impact meet, and I’m excited to keep growing in that direction.

Outside school, I enjoy sports, music, art, and exploring ideas that merge innovation with culture.
